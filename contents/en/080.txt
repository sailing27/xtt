The air tasted of ozone and rust. Maya’s fingers traced the cold, pulsating glyphs on the wall, each one humming with a low, malevolent energy. The labyrinth wasn’t stone; it was pure, corrupted code, its pathways shifting with every breath.

“It’s learning,” she whispered, her voice swallowed by the oppressive silence. A low grind echoed from a dead-end corridor that hadn’t existed a moment before.

A flicker of green light resolved into a single, floating rune. It pulsed once, a silent question. This was the core’s defense mechanism, a puzzle that would either grant passage or collapse the entire sector. Her brother was trapped beyond it. Maya exhaled, her breath fogging in the chilled air. She reached out, not to touch the rune, but to the empty space beside it, where the true key always hid.
