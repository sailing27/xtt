“The stars are singing again.”

“I hear them, too.”

“What do they say?”

“They whisper of forgotten paths. Of doors in the willow roots.”

“Doors to where?”

“To the other side of the sky.”

Her breath fogged the cold glass. Beyond, the constellations shimmered, their light a tangible hum in the still air. It vibrated in her teeth.

“We could go.”

“It’s not for going. It’s for remembering.”

“Remembering what?”

“The before. When the world was soft and new. When we knew the language of stones.”

He placed a hand on the pane. The glass sang a faint, crystalline note. A silver thread of light unwound from the Pleiades, spiraling down through the frost-laced night.

“It’s coming for us.”

“No,” she whispered, her voice full of wonder and dread. “We’re going to it.”
