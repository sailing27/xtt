Leo’s fingers, steady as a surgeon’s, traced the final wire. The old bank vault hadn’t seen a tumble in fifty years, but its heartbeat was a familiar song. He could feel his grandfather’s ghost looking over his shoulder, whispering about pressure and patience.

Across the room, Maya watched the security feed, her breath fogging the monitor’s glass. She wasn’t thinking of the bonds inside the box. She was thinking of the photo tucked in her pocket—her grandmother, standing proudly before this very building the day it opened. This wasn’t theft; it was reclamation.

“Almost there, Leo,” she murmured into her headset, her voice soft as the dust motes dancing in the lamplight.

He smiled, hearing the warmth in her tone. “For the memories,” he whispered back, and the lock surrendered with a satisfying, ancient click.
