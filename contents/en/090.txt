The last box thumped onto the moving truck’s metal floor. Leo stared at the empty space where his bed had been, the ghostly rectangle on the carpet marked by dust. Outside, his best friend, Mateo, shifted his weight on the sidewalk.

“So,” Mateo said, kicking a pebble. It skittered into the storm drain with a final *clink*.

“So,” Leo echoed. The word felt too small for the canyon opening in his chest.

A horn honked. Leo’s dad waved from the driver’s seat. Time was up.

They didn’t do a dramatic hug. Just a hard, quick fist bump, knuckles white. “Send me a postcard from the desert,” Mateo said, his voice cracking just a little.

“Only if you send one back from here,” Leo managed, climbing into the truck. He watched his old street shrink in the side mirror, a blur of green and gray, already becoming a memory.
