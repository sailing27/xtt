Dearest Clara,

The attic yielded a most curious treasure today—a leather-bound journal, its pages smelling of dust and lavender. It belonged to your great-aunt Eleanor, who, it seems, was a secret inventor of useless things.

The first entry reads: *April 3rd, 1912. Success! My automatic sock-darning apparatus is complete. It requires only a team of six mice on a treadmill to operate. Mother is unimpressed.*

I turned a brittle page. *June 10th. The self-stirring teacup works splendidly, though it does tend to wander off the saucer during particularly vigorous stirring.*

Her final entry, dated the eve of her voyage on a ship called the *Titanic*, holds a simple sketch: a complex device labeled “Personal Flotation Waistcoat, Model B.” The page is stained with what looks like saltwater. I think our Eleanor was not so whimsical after all.
