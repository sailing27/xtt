The emergency lights bathed the evacuation corridor in a hellish red glow. Leo’s hand was sweaty in mine, which was frankly disgusting, and I was ninety percent sure this was a drill. But that ten percent? It was screaming.

“If we die,” he panted, shoving a slow-moving freshman aside with a politeness that was pure panic, “I need you to know I never liked your taste in music.”

“If we die,” I shot back, my heart doing a frantic tap-dance against my ribs, “your last thought will be that my playlist is objectively superior.”

We skidded to a halt at the blocked science wing door. The blaring alarm was the only sound for a beat. He looked at me, his usual smirk replaced by something raw and terrifyingly sincere. “Jenna, I—”

“Don’t,” I whispered, the word tasting like metal and fear. “Not unless it’s real.”
