The air in the library was thick with dust and silence, until the book fell. It wasn’t a gentle thump, but a crackle of old parchment and a puff of glittering gold dust that smelled of cinnamon and rain. Elara, who was only supposed to be reshelving geography, froze.

A tiny, iridescent lizard, no bigger than her thumb, skittered from the pages. It blinked up at her with opal eyes.

“You’re late,” it chirped, its voice like tinkling bells. “The portal only holds for three sneezes of a clockwork gnome.”

“Portal?” Elara whispered, her heart a frantic drum against her ribs.

“The Battle of Whimsy Bay is today! They’re short one cannon-loader who believes in six impossible things before breakfast.” The lizard scurried up her arm. “Well? Do you believe?”

Without thinking, Elara scooped up the book. “Implicitly.” She took a deep breath of cinnamon-air and stepped into the shimmering page.
