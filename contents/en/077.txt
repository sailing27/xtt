The letter arrived folded into a paper crane. Its wings were soft, like they’d been handled by a ghost.

*Dearest Finch,*
*Remember the museum’s whispering gallery? How sound traveled along the curve of the dome? I’ve found a similar principle for light. The security lasers in the Starfall Gallery are not barriers, but conductors. Wear the grey silk. It will bend the light around you, make you a shadow in a beam. Meet me when the moon is directly above the central oculus. The sapphire—the “Midnight Meridian”—doesn’t belong in a glass case. It belongs to the sky it mirrors.*

I held the crane to my ear, half-expecting to hear the distant echo of her voice, planning our impossible return. The silk, when I touched it, felt like cool water.
