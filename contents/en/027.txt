The ghost in the attic was not cooperating. It was supposed to moan, rattle chains, and generally be spooky. Instead, it was sobbing into a spectral handkerchief.

“I just wanted to be a mime,” it wailed, a translucent tear rolling down its cheek. “But my parents said it wasn’t a ‘substantial profession’!”

Maya, armed with a vacuum cleaner she’d dubbed ‘The Specter-Sucker 3000,’ lowered her weapon. “So, all the banging last night…?”

“Frustrated interpretive dance!” The ghost gestured dramatically, passing through a support beam. “The critics in the afterlife are brutal.”

Maya sighed, clicking off the vacuum’s ominous hum. “Look, my parents will sell this house if you don’t haunt it properly. Can you maybe… mime being terrifying? Just until Friday?”

The ghost brightened, its form solidifying with purpose. It pretended to be trapped in an invisible box, then mimed climbing a wobbly ladder. It was, Maya had to admit, the most unsettling thing she’d ever seen.
