The inkwell is empty, but I write anyway. My finger traces letters in the dust on the sill. The words glow faintly, a soft blue against the grey.

*Dearest Finn,*

*The crows bring your whispers on the east wind. I hear them in the rustle of dry leaves. They say you’ve reached the Glasswood, where the trees sing in colors only the heart can see. Is it true?*

A shadow passed the window just now. Not a guard—something softer, with moth-wing edges. It left a single, silver scale on the stone.

*They are watching, but they do not understand. The dust remembers. The old magic is in the forgetting.*

My fingertip is growing cold. The blue light is fading. I must be quick.

*Find the tree with the hollow heart. Tell it my name. I will be waiting in the roots of the world.*
