The rain fell in sheets, turning the track into a river of mud. Leo’s lungs burned, each breath a ragged gasp that tasted of iron and wet earth. The stadium lights were just smears of yellow in the gloom, the cheers of the crowd a distant, hollow roar. His legs were lead, his spikes clogged with clay, but the finish line tape was a ghostly white ribbon in the dark.

“You don’t have to do this,” his coach had said, seeing the strain in his eyes before the gun.

But he did. The debt was his father’s, but the running was his own. A final, desperate stride. The tape snapped against his chest, a cold, sharp sting. He didn’t stop, just kept running into the shadows beyond the light, the victory feeling like nothing at all.
