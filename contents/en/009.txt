The rain on the windowpane blurred the neon sign across the street, turning *Joe’s Diner* into a smudge of gold. I traced the old chip in the Formica counter, the one I’d made with a toy car when I was six. The case file in front of me was cold, but the memory was warm.

“Still taking your coffee black, kid?” Joe slid the chipped mug toward me, steam curling into the damp air.

“Some things don’t change.” I took a sip, the bitter heat a familiar anchor.

The file said my client was a liar. But her photo showed her holding a kitten, her smile exactly like my sister’s used to be. Sometimes the truth isn’t in the facts, but in the feeling you get right here, in the pit of your stomach. I closed the folder. Some cases you take with your head. This one, I’d take with my heart.
