“We’re out of gas.”

“I can see that, Leo.”

“No, I mean *out*. The needle’s been on E for twenty miles.”

Maya stared at the empty highway stretching into the Nevada heat haze. The asphalt shimmered. The only sound was the engine’s dying click and the buzz of flies against the windshield.

“You said you’d check in Baker.”

“You said you’d pay for half the tank.”

Silence, thick and hot. A tumbleweed bounced across the road. Leo’s knuckles were white on the wheel.

“Now what?”

Maya dug a hand into her jeans pocket, pulled out three crumpled dollars and some lint. She held it out on her palm.

Leo looked at the money, then at her face. He let out a slow breath, the fight draining out of him. “Now we walk.”
