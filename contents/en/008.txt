The rain fell in sheets, turning the track to mud. Leo’s lungs burned, each breath a ragged gasp that tasted of iron and wet earth. The stadium lights were blurred halos in the downpour, the cheers of the crowd a distant, muffled roar.

He could see the finish line tape, a ghostly ribbon in the gloom. And he could see Martinez beside him, stride for stride, their shoes slapping the same punishing rhythm.

“Not… this time,” Leo ground out, the words lost to the storm and the fire in his legs.

This was the last qualifying race. Fourth place got nothing. Third was a ticket out. His mother’s tired eyes flashed behind his own—the second job, the silent hope. He dug deeper, into a place beyond pain, and found one final, desperate surge. The tape waited.
