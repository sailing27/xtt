The old theater smelled of dust and forgotten velvet. Leo traced a finger along the ornate carvings of the locked display case, his breath fogging the glass that held the Star of Arden, a brooch that caught the dim light and fractured it into a hundred warm, amber points. It was the last piece his grandmother had crafted before the shop was sold.

“It’s just a loan,” he whispered to the silence, the lock picks cold in his palm. The floorboards creaked a familiar protest, a sound from a hundred childhood afternoons hiding backstage. He wasn’t stealing a jewel; he was reclaiming a memory, a fragment of her laughter trapped behind glass. The first pick slid in with a soft *click* that echoed like a heartbeat in the hollow dark.
