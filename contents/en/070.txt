The rain made everything smell like wet concrete and regret. Maya watched it streak the window of the bus, blurring the neon sign of the pawn shop across the street. Her knuckles were white around the strap of her backpack, feeling the weight of the laptop inside—her brother’s, not hers.

Two seats back, Leo counted the change in his pocket for the third time. Seventy-three cents. The bus fare was two dollars. His stomach growled, a hollow echo of the dinner he wouldn’t have.

The driver, Maria, sighed into the silence. She saw the kid’s anxious reflection in her mirror, the tight set of his shoulders. Her shift ended in twenty minutes. Without a word, she reached over and flipped the fare box switch to ‘Out of Service’ for the next stop. A small mercy in a hard city.
