The attic smelled of cedar and forgotten summers. My fingers brushed the dusty spine of the old journal, its leather cover warm from the sunbeam cutting through the dormer window. It was Gran’s, from when she was my age.

*July 12th, 1963. The key to the blue door isn’t in the kitchen drawer anymore. I saw him move it. He thinks I don’t watch.*

I turned the brittle page, my heart a sudden, frantic bird against my ribs. The blue door was in our basement, always locked. Dad said it just led to old pipes.

*July 14th. I found where he hides it now. Under the loose floorboard, third step from the top. I have to know what’s behind it.*

The house was silent. I knew every creak of that staircase. I knew I would go down there.
