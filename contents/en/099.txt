The van’s engine screamed like a dying animal. In the passenger seat, Leo clutched a stolen thermos of radioactive-looking soup. “Remind me again why we’re fleeing a crime scene with a vegan’s lunch?”

“It’s evidence!” Mara yelled, swerving around a dumpster. “And Mrs. Henderson is not a vegan, she’s a warlock. That’s a soul-stew.”

In the back, twelve-year-old Finn desperately tried to spoon the glowing broth into a containment jar. The van hit a pothole. A glob of stew hit the ceiling, where it sizzled and spelled out the word “REPENT” in angry, phosphorescent letters.

Leo stared at the dripping message. “Our last foster family just made us do chores.”

Mara met his eyes in the rearview mirror, a wild grin splitting her face. “See? Upgrade.”

Another police siren wailed behind them. Finn sealed the jar. “Family meeting: we’re keeping the van, right?”
