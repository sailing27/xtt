The shelling had stopped. In the sudden quiet, Leo’s ears rang.

“Think they’re gone?” he whispered to the private beside him, a boy named Finch who collected bottle caps.

Finch didn’t look up from the mud. “Doubt it. They’re just… thinking.”

“About what?”

“Dinner, probably. Or the weather. I’m thinking about my grandmother’s lemon cake.”

A distant *crump* echoed. Dirt rained down. Leo brushed it from his hair. “They’re back.”

“Told you,” Finch said, finally meeting his eye. A faint smile touched his grimy face. “They were thinking about the weather. Decided they didn’t like it.”

Another shell screamed overhead. They both flinched, shoulders touching in the narrow trench.

“Lemon cake,” Leo said, closing his eyes. “With the powdered sugar?”

“Glazed,” Finch corrected, his voice steady. “Always glazed.”
