The note was damp from his palm. *Meet me at the old bridge. Midnight. Don’t be seen.* Kai’s handwriting, always a frantic scrawl, sent a jolt through her.

Across town, Kai watched the clock, each tick a hammer blow. He’d seen the men in dark coats asking questions. They’d find her next if he didn’t act.

Lena slipped through the moonlit woods, the cold air sharp in her lungs. Twigs snapped like gunshots. Was this about the letters? The secret they’d promised to keep?

He saw her first, a shadow detaching from the pines. Relief and terror warred in his chest. “They know,” he breathed, reaching for her hand. Its warmth was the only solid thing in the spinning dark.

“Then we run,” she said, her fingers tightening around his. “Together.”
