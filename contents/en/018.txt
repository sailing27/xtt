The last flare sputtered out, leaving only the cold, indifferent stars. Maya’s fingers, numb inside her torn gloves, fumbled with the empty ration pack. Across the dying embers, Leo watched the horizon where the rescue ship had vanished hours ago, its signal lost in the storm.

“They’ll circle back,” he said, his voice raspy. It was a statement, not a question.

Maya didn’t answer. She listened to the wind howl across the frozen plain, a sound that had become the voice of this alien world. She thought of Earth’s constant hum—traffic, voices, life. Here, there was only this vast, beautiful silence, waiting to swallow them.

“Look,” Leo whispered.

A single green shoot, impossibly fragile, pushed through the ice near her boot. A bittersweet ache filled her chest. They might not survive the night, but something here, on this unforgiving rock, stubbornly would.
