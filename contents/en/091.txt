The first time I saw a dragon, I threw up on my commanding officer’s boots. Not from fear, mind you. From the smell. They don’t tell you that in the recruitment pamphlets. They show the gleaming scales and the heroic riders, not the fact that a firedrake’s breath smells like a slaughterhouse left in the sun.

“Get up, Private,” Captain Vane sighed, scraping his boot in the mud. “It’s just a bit of sulfur and digested goat.”

Now, three months in the trenches, the smell is just part of the background noise. Like the constant thunder of wings and the distant screams. Yesterday, my bunkmate, Jax, got singed bald. He spent the evening trying to sculpt his remaining eyebrow hairs into something fashionable. We laughed until we cried. Out here, you either find the joke or the joke finds you. Usually, it finds you crispy.
