The whispers in the wallpaper started again last Tuesday. They’re not mean, just chatty, offering advice on my geometry homework and critiques of my dad’s lasagna. I told my friend Maya about them at lunch.

“They say you should wear the green socks tomorrow,” I mentioned, stirring my chocolate milk.

Maya blinked. “The whispers?”

“The ones in the floral print by the ceiling. They have strong opinions on footwear.”

She smiled her careful smile, the one that doesn’t reach her eyes. “Right. Of course.”

I didn’t tell her the rest—how the violets in the pattern sometimes untwist their stems when no one’s looking, or how the whispers promised my missing cat would come back if I just stopped telling people about them. So I’ve stopped. Mostly. But the green socks *were* a perfect choice.
