The last box is taped shut. My room echoes now, just bare walls and dust bunnies where my posters used to be. I found your old friendship bracelet, the one with the frayed green thread, tucked behind my dresser. I’d forgotten I still had it.

Remember how we made them in your backyard, swearing we’d never take them off? Mine lasted a week before the shower turned it into a soggy mess. Yours stayed on for years, until it just… wasn’t there one day. You never said why.

I’m holding it now. Part of me wants to toss it in the “donate” box. The other part wants to slip it into my pocket, a secret relic to carry into this new, unknown city. I can still smell the summer grass, hear your laugh. I put it in my pocket. The past is light, but it still has weight.
