The executioner’s axe felt heavier than usual. “It’s the damp,” he grumbled to his apprentice, who was busy being sick behind the chopping block. “Swells the grain.”

In the crowd, Lady Eleanor adjusted her lace cuffs, bored. “Must he dawdle so? I’m missing my lemon cakes.”

The condemned thief, one-eyed Jack, squinted at the gray sky. A single drop of rain hit his forehead. “Typical,” he muttered. “Couldn’t even arrange a sunny day for my departure.”

The apprentice wiped his mouth. “Is it always this… messy?”

“Only when they squirm,” the executioner sighed, hefting the blade. “Now hold his shoulders, lad. And for heaven’s sake, aim your sickness the other way.”

Jack chuckled. “Cheer up, son. In a moment, my view improves dramatically.”
