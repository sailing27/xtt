The library’s silence was a living thing, broken only by the soft *thump* of returned books. That’s why the scream was so shocking. I found Miss Elara, our normally unflappable librarian, pale as parchment, pointing a trembling finger. Her prized first edition of *The Phantom’s Riddle* was gone. In its place on the velvet stand sat a single, perfect origami swan.

“It was here ten minutes ago,” she whispered. “No one came in but the book club.”

My heart did a funny little flip. I’d seen Leo from chemistry class fiddling with paper earlier, his fingers moving with strange, precise grace. But accusing him felt too simple, like the first chapter of a mystery where the obvious suspect is never the one. I picked up the swan. A single word was inked inside a hidden fold: *borrowed*.
