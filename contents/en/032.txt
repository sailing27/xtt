The city lights bleed orange through the grimy window. I found your last letter, the one you never sent, tucked inside my old copy of *The Bell Jar*. The paper smells like the damp basement where you wrote it.

*I can’t do this anymore, Eli. The pretending. Mom’s screaming again. Dad’s bottle is empty. I feel like I’m made of glass and everyone is waiting for the crack.*

I trace the frantic ink, the tear-smudged words. You wrote this the night before you left. I should have known. I should have heard the silence between your sentences.

My phone buzzes—a new message from an unknown number. Just two words: *I’m sorry.*

The static in my chest becomes a roar. I type back, fingers trembling against the cracked screen. *Where are you?* I hit send. The message fails to deliver.
