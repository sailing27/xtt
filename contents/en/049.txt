The rain tapped a Morse code against my office window. I was polishing the old brass magnifying glass my dad left me when she walked in. Twelve years old, maybe. Dripping wet, clutching a soggy paper bag.

“I need you to find him,” she said, her voice small but steady.

“Who’s missing?”

“My dog. Buster. He’s a terrier. Mostly ears.” She placed the bag on my desk. Inside were two slightly melted gingersnaps. “Payment in advance.”

I looked at the cookies, then at her hopeful, rain-streaked face. I hadn’t had a real case in months. Just shadows and old regrets.

“Best retainer I’ve ever seen,” I said, grabbing my hat. “Let’s go find those ears.” The rain didn’t feel so cold anymore.
