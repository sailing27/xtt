The old lighthouse had been empty for years, until Leo arrived with a backpack and a broken compass. He saw it as a temporary shelter.

Maya noticed the flicker of candlelight from her window across the cove. She packed a basket with bread and jam, curiosity tugging her toward the shore.

From his fishing boat, old Mr. Henderson saw them both. He remembered being young and adrift.

“The door’s open,” Leo called, surprised by the knock. Maya stepped in, her offering held out like a peace treaty. “I brought breakfast.”

Later, Mr. Henderson joined them, sharing stories of storms and shipwrecks over mugs of strong tea. The lighthouse beam swept over the dark water, and for the first time, Leo didn’t feel its light was searching for something lost, but rather, signaling that something had been found.
