The engine screamed as the needle kissed the red. Rain slashed the windshield, turning the world into a smear of neon and asphalt. In the passenger seat, Leo clutched the door handle, knuckles white.

“He’s still back there,” he breathed, watching the single headlight in the rearview.

“I know,” Mara snapped, downshifting into a curve. The tires protested, a sharp smell of burnt rubber filling the car. She’d been driving for seven hours straight, her jaw a tight line. This wasn’t the adventure they’d planned. This was flight.

A truck horn blared as she swerved across two lanes. Leo flinched. “We could just pull over. Explain.”

Mara’s eyes, reflected in the glass, were hard and ancient. “Some things you don’t explain, Leo. You just outrun them.” She stomped the gas, and the night swallowed them whole.
