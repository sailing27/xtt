The air in the attic tasted of dust and forgotten summers. Leo held his breath, fingers tracing the rough edge of the floorboard he’d just pried up. The space below was a pocket of shadow, but something glinted—a small, tarnished key. His heart hammered against his ribs, a frantic drumbeat echoing the storm outside. This was it. The thing his grandfather had whispered about, the secret that had made his voice go thin and strained.

“Don’t go looking for locks, boy,” he’d said, just last week. “Some doors are better left shut.”

But Leo was tired of whispers. The key was cold in his palm, heavier than it should be. Downstairs, the front door groaned open. He wasn’t supposed to be up here. He had seconds to decide: hide the key, or see what it opened.
