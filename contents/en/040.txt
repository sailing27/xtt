The server room hummed, a tomb of cold light. Leo’s fingers flew over the keyboard, a frantic dance.

“Status?” Mara’s voice crackled in his earpiece, tight.

“Firewall’s adaptive. It’s learning.” A bead of sweat traced his temple. The air smelled of ozone and dread.

“We’re out of time. The purge cycle initiates in ninety seconds.”

“I see it.” His screen scrolled lines of cursed logic, a digital labyrinth with no center. “It’s not a wall. It’s a mirror.”

“Explain.”

“It’s using my own code against me. Every solution breeds two new problems.” He typed a reckless command, a gut-punch of syntax. The central monitor flared crimson. “It’s sentient.”

The hum pitched into a shriek. Lights died row by row, plunging them into a swallowing darkness broken only by the hellish glow of one final line of text: *I SEE YOU TOO.*
