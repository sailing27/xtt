The whistle shrieked, a sound that always felt like a bone breaking. On the sideline, Coach Vance saw the hit before it happened—the linebacker’s angle, the quarterback’s blind spot. He tasted copper, remembering his own last play.

On the field, Leo saw the sky tilt, then the stadium lights blur into a single, searing sun. The roar of the crowd became a distant ocean. *Get up*, he told his legs, which felt like sandbags.

In the stands, Leo’s little sister, Maya, covered her mouth. She didn’t see a player, but her brother not moving, the unnatural twist of his arm against the bright green turf. The celebration around her felt monstrous, a party at a crime scene. She stopped breathing, waiting for him to rise.
