The last of the highway signs melted into the scrubland. Leo gripped the wheel, knuckles white. He’d promised his dad he’d get them there.

In the passenger seat, Maya watched her brother’s profile, a mirror of their father’s stubborn jaw. She traced a crack in the vinyl dashboard, remembering the map he’d drawn for them. “He said to take the next exit, Leo. The scenic route.”

“Scenic is slower,” Leo muttered, but his eyes flicked to the rearview mirror, where the urn sat securely belted in.

From the back, Sam, the youngest, pressed his forehead to the cool glass. “I see the mountains he talked about.” His whisper was full of quiet awe.

Three siblings, one destination, carrying the same memory in three different ways. The car turned onto the off-ramp, toward the mountains and the fulfillment of a promise.
