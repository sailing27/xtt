The attic smelled of cedar and forgotten things, but to Leo, it was a kingdom. He’d found the old armchair first, its velvet worn soft. Then came the mismatched lamp with the tasseled shade, casting a warm, buttery circle on the floorboards. He’d dragged up a crate for a table.

“It’s missing something,” he murmured to the dust motes dancing in the light.

The creak of the attic stairs announced Elara, his new foster-sister. She held a chipped teapot and two mugs. “For important meetings,” she said, her voice barely a whisper.

Leo arranged the mugs on the crate. Outside, the rain began to patter on the roof, sealing them in their cozy, cobwebbed world. He poured imaginary tea. “Welcome to the committee of lost things,” he said. Elara’s small, real smile was the final piece.
