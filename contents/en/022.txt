The air tasted of ozone and rust. Kael’s fingers trembled on the coolant valve.

“Status.”
“Pressure’s dropping. Fast.”
“How fast?”
“Three minutes. Maybe two.”

A deep shudder ran through the station’s skeletal frame. A warning light painted the cramped conduit in pulsing crimson.

“The seal won’t hold,” Kael said, voice flat.
“We knew that.” Jax didn’t look up from her scanner. “Primary coupling is fused. Can’t reroute.”
“So we lose the module.”
“We lose the module. And the cargo.”
“The company will have our skins.”
“Only if we’re alive to collect the demerits.”

Another shudder, sharper. A hiss of escaping gas became a shriek.

“Time?”
“Gone.”

Kael met Jax’s eyes. No fear, just a weary calculation. They’d gambled on a quick fix. They’d lost.

“Brace.”
He slammed the emergency purge lever. The world dissolved into a roar of white and cold.
