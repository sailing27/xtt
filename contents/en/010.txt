The attic smelled of cedar and forgotten things. I was supposed to be looking for Grandma’s old photo albums, but my fingers brushed against the worn leather of my old softball mitt instead. I hadn’t seen it since the season ended—since I quit the team.

“You’ll regret it,” Mom had said softly, but she didn’t push.

Now, I slipped my hand inside. The pocket was still molded to my fingers. I could almost hear the *thwack* of a perfect catch, feel the summer sun on my neck. A fine layer of dust coated the laces. Part of me wanted to shake it off, to run downstairs and ask if tryouts were still open. The other part just held it tighter, knowing some choices, once made, can’t be undone. The mitt went back into the box, a little heavier than before.
