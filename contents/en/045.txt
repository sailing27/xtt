The air in the cavern tasted of damp stone and forgotten magic. Lyra’s fingers trembled as they hovered over the final rune. One wrong glyph, and the bridge of woven light spanning the chasm would unravel.

“It’s singing,” she whispered, the melody a vibration in her bones.

Her companion, a fox with eyes like smoldering coals, nudged her hand. “The song is a thread. Pull it, and the path appears. Hesitate, and the dark drinks it whole.”

Lyra closed her eyes, letting the crystalline hum fill her. She didn’t carve the rune; she let it spill from her fingertips like ink from a dream. The bridge solidified, a ribbon of captured moonlight. As she stepped onto its impossible surface, the fox at her heels, the cavern’s shadows sighed and began, very softly, to bloom.
