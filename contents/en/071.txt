The sky above the trenches was the color of a faded bruise, tinged with the unnatural green of distant flares. Private Arlo Finch wasn't watching the sky, however. He was watching a ladybug, its shell a perfect polished ruby, navigate the splintered wood of the parapet.

“It’s going the wrong way, bug,” he whispered, his breath fogging in the dawn chill. “The roses are back *there*.”

A shell screamed overhead, the earth trembling in response. Dirt pattered down like black rain. The ladybug opened its wings, a flash of impossible crimson against the mud, and flew—not toward the ruined village, but straight across No Man’s Land, toward the enemy trench.

Arlo watched until the speck of red vanished into the gloom. For the first time in weeks, he felt a strange, whimsical hope. If something that delicate could cross that terrible space, perhaps letters from home could, too.
