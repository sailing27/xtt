The first note appeared taped to my locker, written in glitter gel pen: "Your shoelaces are untied." They weren't. The second was slipped into my math book: "Your pencil is about to break." It snapped mid-equation. The third arrived yesterday, scrawled on a napkin beside my lunch tray: "You have spinach in your teeth." I didn't even eat spinach.

Today's was different. A single, crisp notecard waited on my desk. The handwriting was the same looping script, but the message made my breath catch. "I know who took the headmaster's prized garden gnome, Bartholomew. Meet me at the old oak at sunset. Come alone. P.S. Your left sock has a hole in the toe."

I checked. It did.
