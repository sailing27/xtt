The rain had finally stopped, which was a shame. It meant the mosquitoes were back. I crouched in the damp hollow of a fallen log, my stomach performing a symphony of complaint. I’d been tracking a rabbit for an hour, only to watch it vanish into a bramble thicket with what I swear was a mocking flick of its tail.

“Fantastic,” I muttered to the empty woods. “Just brilliant.”

My last protein bar was a memory. My boots were squelching with a sound that suggested amphibian life. Survival, I decided, was mostly just being spectacularly, profoundly annoyed. Not by the grand, dramatic dangers—those were almost a relief—but by the relentless, petty inconveniences. The universe wasn’t trying to kill me. It was just being a real jerk about everything.
