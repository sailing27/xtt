The rain on the windowpane blurred the neon sign across the street, turning *Joe’s Diner* into a smudge of pink and gold. I traced the path of a single drop with my finger, remembering the first time I sat in this cracked vinyl booth. I was twelve, tailing my dad on a real case. He’d bought me a cherry phosphate and said, “Kid, the best clues aren’t in the files. They’re in the quiet moments between.”

Now his old fedora sat on the seat beside me, the brim slightly frayed. The case file in front of me was cold, but the memory was warm. A waitress refilled my coffee. “He’d be proud, you know,” she said softly, placing the check facedown. I nodded, leaving the hat on the seat as I stood. Some clues you’re meant to carry with you.
