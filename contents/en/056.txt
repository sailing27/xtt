The thing about being the school’s designated “problem case” is the sheer monotony of it. Another Tuesday, another summons to the vice principal’s office, which smelled perpetually of industrial lemon cleaner and quiet despair.

“We’re concerned, Leo,” Vice Principal Garrity droned, not looking up from my file. “Your ‘artistic expression’ on the chemistry lab tables.”

“It was a diagram of existential dread,” I clarified. “The acetone was just a medium.”

He sighed, a sound like a deflating tire. “Your father will be notified.”

I watched a fly struggle against the windowpane. That was the real performance art right there. We were both trapped in a beige box, buzzing pointlessly against an invisible barrier. The only difference was, the fly probably didn’t have to go home later and pretend the disappointment in his dad’s eyes was just a trick of the light.
