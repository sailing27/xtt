The old map trembled in Leo’s hands, its edges whispering against his calloused fingertips. “It’s real,” he breathed, the ink of the forgotten forest seeming to pulse with a faint, golden light.

Across the village, his sister Maya felt a sudden, warm pull in her chest, like a string tied to her heart had been plucked. She dropped her gardening trowel, soil crumbling from her hands, and looked instinctively toward Leo’s attic window. He was always finding lost things, but this felt different. This felt like being found.

In the attic, Leo traced the route with one finger. “We have to go tomorrow,” he murmured to the dusty air.

Maya, still in her sun-warmed garden, nodded to no one. “We’re going tomorrow,” she whispered back, the certainty settling over her like a familiar, cherished cloak. The adventure wasn’t just ahead; it was already stitching them together.
