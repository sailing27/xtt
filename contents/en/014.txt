The gas station coffee tasted like burnt rubber and regret. I scribbled on a napkin while Dad argued with the map.

*Day 3. Arizona. Dad’s trying to navigate by “gut feeling.” My gut feels like we’re lost.*

“The interstate is a soul-crushing concrete river, kiddo,” he’d declared, folding the official map away. “We find the real America out here.” Out here was just dust and heat and the smell of overheated engine coolant.

*Mom would’ve had us at a proper hotel by now.*

He slid back into the driver’s seat, his smile tight. “Adventure, right?”

I just nodded, crumpling the napkin in my fist. My words were for me now. The road unspooled ahead, a shimmering gray line into nowhere, and his silence felt heavier than the desert air.
