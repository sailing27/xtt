The world is made of glass and whispers. I move through it like a ghost, my fingers brushing the cool, smooth surfaces of things I’m not supposed to touch. The vault wasn’t a door; it was a sigh, a final exhalation of security giving way. Inside, it’s not about the jewels, cold and glittering like captured starlight. It’s about the silence, the perfect, stolen quiet at the heart of a screaming city.

“It’s just a job,” Leo always says, his voice gritty with the dust of a thousand alleys.

But he’s wrong. This is a sacrament. My palm closes around a single, flawless sapphire. Its chill seeps into my skin, a secret winter. In this moment of perfect theft, I don’t feel like a thief. I feel like the last magician in a world that’s forgotten how to dream.
