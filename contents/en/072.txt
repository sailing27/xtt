The rain fell in sheets, turning the city’s grime into a slick, black mirror. Leo pressed himself against the alley wall, the stolen data drive burning a hole in his pocket. He’d promised her he was done with this life.

A shadow detached itself from the deeper darkness. “You have something of mine.” The voice was silk over steel.

“It’s over, Mara,” Leo said, his own voice raw. “This ends tonight.”

She stepped into the weak light, her eyes holding the same electric charge as the storm. “You took it for her, didn’t you? The girl from the library.”

He didn’t deny it. The memory of a shared laugh, a brush of hands over dusty books, was the only warmth left in him. “She’s worth more than all your schemes.”

Mara’s smile was a knife-cut. “Let’s see if you’re worth dying for, then.” She moved, a blur of lethal grace.
