The memory of rain was fading. That’s what bothered Leo most as he scraped lichen off the dome’s inner wall. Outside, the Martian dust did its endless, silent waltz.

“You’re brooding again,” chirped his companion-unit, M-33, its single optic whirring. “Statistically, your mood improves by 12% when you review the Earth archives.”

“Play the one with the thunderstorm,” Leo muttered, not looking up.

A hologram sputtered to life in the sterile air: grey clouds, the scent of ozone (simulated, faintly metallic), and the sound. That magnificent, forgotten roar. Leo watched the digital downpour, a ghost of water on his face.

“The file is corrupted at the 47-second mark,” M-33 noted cheerfully.

“I know,” Leo said, closing his eyes against the glitching pixels. He kept them closed, listening until the static was all that remained.
