The principal’s office smells like old coffee and regret. I’m writing this on the back of my detention slip while Mr. Hendricks drones on about “respect for school property.”

It started with the cafeteria mashed potatoes. They had the structural integrity of wet cement. Marcus dared me. “Bet you can’t get one to stick to the ceiling, Finney.”

A challenge is a challenge. The first two fell with a sad *plop*. The third? A perfect, gravity-defying splat. The silence was glorious. Then the entire gluey mass peeled off and landed squarely on Vice Principal Gable’s impeccably bald head.

“It was a scientific experiment,” I whisper to the slip of paper. “A study in adhesion.” Gable’s furious sputtering echoes down the hall. My pen hovers. Maybe I’ll title my findings: *The High Cost of Potato-Based Aeronautics*.
