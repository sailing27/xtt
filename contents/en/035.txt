The rain fell like a thousand tiny hammers on the tin roof of the old observatory. Inside, the air smelled of ozone, damp wool, and one very dead astronomy professor. He was slumped over his telescope, a single, perfect domino—the expensive kind—resting on the star chart beside him.

“He was always so dramatic,” sighed Lydia, adjusting her cat-eye glasses. “A domino? Really?”

Her twin brother, Leo, poked the professor’s shoulder with a pencil. “It’s not a calling card. It’s a taunt. The killer thinks we’re playing a game.”

A flash of lightning illuminated the room, glinting off the lenses of the telescope. For a split second, Leo saw it: a smudge on the eyepiece, not from a finger, but from the edge of a domino tile. The killer had looked back at their work. The game, it seemed, was far from over.
