The city slept beneath a dome of perpetual twilight, its towers like broken teeth against the violet sky. Elara pressed her palm to the cool glass of the observation deck, her breath fogging the view of the silent streets far below. They called this hour the “Stillness,” when the air-purifiers hummed their lowest note and the world seemed to hold its breath.

“It’s quieter than usual,” she whispered, not to anyone in particular.

A single, silver leaf—a relic from the Old World, they said—drifted past the window, carried on an impossible breeze from the ventilation shafts. No trees grew in the undercity. Her heart gave a strange, hopeful lurch. The leaf was a message, a tear in the seamless fabric of their controlled existence. She watched it spiral down into the gloom, a tiny rebellion against the gray.
