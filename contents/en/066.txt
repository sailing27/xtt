The rain fell in a steady, gray curtain, blurring the world beyond the bus shelter. Leo clutched the slightly damp, crumpled paper in his pocket—the acceptance letter to a school three states away. He’d been waiting for this moment for years, dreaming of escape from his sleepy town. Now, standing here with the metallic scent of wet pavement filling his nose, the victory felt hollow.

Next to him, his best friend Sam stared at the same rain, a half-eaten, soggy bag of gummy worms between them. “So,” Sam said, not looking at him. “You’re really going to become a fancy artist and forget how to draw stupid cartoons on napkins?”

Leo watched a single red gummy worm slide off the bag and dissolve into a puddle. “Never,” he said, his voice thick. The word hung in the damp air, a fragile promise against the drumming rain.
