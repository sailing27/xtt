The rain on the windowpane smeared the streetlights into golden tears. Leo watched them trace paths, his breath fogging the glass. In the reflection, his sister’s empty bed was a dark rectangle.

“She’s just sleepwalking again,” his father’s voice, frayed at the edges, drifted up the stairs. “She always comes back.”

But Leo had seen the note, the one hidden under her pillow. *The whispering in the walls has a name now.*

Across town, Mara stood in the overgrown garden of the old Vicarage. The air tasted of wet soil and rust. A faint hum vibrated up through the soles of her shoes, a frequency that matched the one she’d started hearing in her dreams.

“I’m here,” she whispered to the crumbling bricks, and a shadow in the high window shifted in reply.
