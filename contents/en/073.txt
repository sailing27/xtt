I press my forehead against the cool glass, my breath fogging a small circle in the grime. Below, the city is a grid of sterile white light, but up here, on the forbidden rooftop, I can almost see the stars.

“Remember how they used to look?” My sister’s voice is a whisper beside me. She’s holding the old, cracked photo of our parents, taken under a sky full of them.

“Like spilled sugar,” I say, the memory surfacing like a dream. I can almost smell the cut grass from that long-ago picnic, feel the summer air, not this processed chill.

A siren wails far below. We don’t flinch. For these few stolen minutes, the ghost of that old sky is ours, and it’s enough.
