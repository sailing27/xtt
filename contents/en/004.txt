“We need to talk.”

“Now?” I didn’t look up from my calculus. The numbers were a safe harbor.

“Yes, now.” Her voice was a taut wire. “Look at me.”

I finally did. Her knuckles were white on the doorframe. The air smelled of rain and impending storm.

“I found the letters, Maya. The ones from the art school.”

My pencil stopped. The safe harbor vanished. “Those are private.”

“Private? You lied. You told them no. You told *us* no.”

“It’s complicated.”

“Un-complicate it.” A tear tracked through the dust on her cheek. “Tell me why you gave up your dream before you even tried.”

The words clogged my throat. Fear. The sheer, map-less terror of wanting something so much it could break you.

“I was scared,” I whispered.

She stepped into the room. “So be scared. But don’t be silent.”
