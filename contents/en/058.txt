“We’re out of water.”

“I know.”

“The map’s wrong. The spring’s not here.”

“I know that, too.”

My throat felt like crumpled sandpaper. I watched Leo scrape at the dry creek bed with a flat stone. Dust puffed up, coating his sunburned arms. The silence between us was a third person, heavy and thirsty.

“Remember the pond behind your house?” he said, not looking up. “The tadpoles?”

“You fell in. Your mom was furious.”

A ghost of a smile touched his cracked lips. “Water was everywhere then.”

I stared at the barren wash, the bleached sky. Our canteens were empty, but the memory was cool and full. It was something. For now, it had to be enough.

“Come on,” I said, standing. “We keep moving.”
