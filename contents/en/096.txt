The pills make the whispers softer, but they never really go away. I take them anyway, because Mom’s eyes get that fragile, hopeful look when I swallow the little white circle. “Feeling better today, sweetie?” she asks, her hand cool on my forehead.

“Much,” I lie, forcing a smile. The truth is a tangled knot in my chest. The man in the corner of my room hasn’t left; he just blurs at the edges now, a smudged charcoal sketch of a person. Sometimes I think he’s trying to warn me about something. Other times, I think he’s just waiting.

I can’t tell Mom. Her hope is a thin, precious thread. So I nod and say the room feels quieter, all while the silent figure watches, and I wonder which one of us is actually here.
