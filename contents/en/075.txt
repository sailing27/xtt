The rain fell on Ravenswood Manor like a chorus of tiny, disapproving sighs. Inside the grand library, the air was thick with dust and suspicion. Great-Uncle Alistair was most definitely dead, slumped in his favorite wingback chair with a look of profound irritation frozen on his face.

“He always said he’d die before letting anyone touch his stamp collection,” murmured Cousin Felicity, examining a letter opener with theatrical curiosity.

“A sentiment someone appears to have taken quite literally,” I replied, eyeing the peculiar, mint-green stain on his cravat. It smelled faintly of licorice and betrayal. The will, promising everything to his pet parrot, sat prominently on the desk. Every relative in the room had motive, and the parrot, currently squawking “Guilty! Guilty!” from its gilded cage, was the only one without an alibi.
