The gallows creaked in the morning mist, a sound like a rusty door in a haunted house. I stood in the crowd, my new apprentice cap itching terribly.

“Quite the spectacle, eh, boy?” Master Fitch elbowed me, his breath smelling of yesterday’s mutton. “Pay attention. This is where poor craftsmanship gets you.”

The condemned man, a thatcher, was having the noose adjusted. “It’s slipping!” he complained, voice cracking.

“Nonsense,” said the hangman, giving the rope a professional tug. “My knots are impeccable. You’ll die with the best.”

I watched, my stomach churning. The thatcher’s final words were a critique of the roof beam above him. “The joinery is shoddy! It’ll rot within a year!”

The trapdoor fell with a splintering shriek. He was right, of course. The beam did sound terribly unsound.
