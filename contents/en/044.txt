The air in the bus terminal tasted of diesel and stale pretzels. Maya clutched the ticket in her damp palm, the paper edge biting into her skin. Her duffel bag, stuffed with everything she dared to take, felt impossibly light.

“Final call for Greyhound 227 to Denver,” the staticky speaker announced.

She glanced back at the automatic doors, half-expecting to see her father’s truck screech to the curb. The fluorescent lights hummed, casting a sickly glow on the cracked linoleum. This was it. The point of no return was a scuffed yellow line on the floor.

“You getting on, kid?” the driver called, impatience tightening his voice.

Maya took a sharp breath that didn’t quite reach her lungs. “Yeah,” she said, her voice a stranger’s. “I’m getting on.” She stepped over the line.
