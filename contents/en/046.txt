The smell of burnt popcorn and desperation hung in the air of the community center basement. I was wedged between a sniffling tween and a guy who kept muttering about his “artisanal sourdough starter.” This was my life now: Tuesday Night Teen Support Group for “Unusual Hobbies.”

“And what brings you here, Leo?” the facilitator asked, her smile painfully sincere.

I cleared my throat. “I, uh, communicate with pigeons. Not like, cooing at them. Full conversations. Strategic alliances.”

The room went silent, save for the hum of the faulty fluorescent light. The sourdough guy scoffed. “Pigeons? Please. My starter has a more complex personality.”

Then, from the high window, a soft *coo*. A sleek gray head appeared, beady eyes locking onto mine. He dropped a single french fry onto the linoleum with a quiet *plink*.

“That’s Gerald,” I said, not bothering to hide my grin. “He says your starter is under-proofed.”
