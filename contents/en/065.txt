The dragon’s hoard was, frankly, disappointing. Not a single gleaming coin or enchanted sword—just a cavern floor littered with mismatched socks, a few tarnished spoons, and a truly alarming number of hairbrushes tangled with silver and copper strands.

“It’s a magpie,” I whispered, my grand quest crumbling into absurdity. “A giant, fire-breathing magpie.”

A rustle came from the shadows, followed by a low, rumbling sigh. “They have a nice shine,” the dragon said, its voice like grinding stones and old regret. One great, amber eye blinked open, fixing on me. “The spoons, especially. Remind me of the lake by my first nest.” It nudged a tarnished teaspoon with a claw the size of my leg. “You’re here for treasure, I suppose. Take a sock. The blue one has excellent elasticity.”

I didn’t take the sock. I sat down. And for the first time in centuries, someone listened.
