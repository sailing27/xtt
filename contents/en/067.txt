“So, you’re a ghost.”

“Specter, technically. There’s a difference in the spectral density.”

“Right. And you’re haunting my locker because…?”

“Prime metaphysical real estate. The lingering scent of old gym socks and despair creates a powerful anchor.”

I blinked. A faint, pearlescent teenager was wedged between my algebra book and a moldy banana. “You can’t stay.”

“I’ll make it worth your while.” He gestured, and a single, perfect cheat sheet materialized on my trig homework. “I was an ‘A’ student. In 1987.”

“Tempting. But no.”

“I can also make the principal’s hairpiece levitate during assembly.”

I paused, considering the potential for beautiful, beautiful chaos. The bell rang, a sharp clang that echoed in the suddenly empty hall. His translucent form flickered with what looked like hope.

“Fine,” I sighed, shoving the banana deeper. “But you handle any ectoplasmic mess. And the 80s references? Keep them to a minimum.”
