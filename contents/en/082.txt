The first time I saw the stars, I cried. Not the pale, washed-out pinpricks from Earth, but the real ones—a riot of color and light from the viewport of the generation ship. My great-great-grandparents boarded this vessel. I’ll die on it, never setting foot on the new world it journeys toward.

“It’s beautiful, isn’t it?” Mom’s voice was soft beside me, her hand warm on my shoulder.

“It is,” I whispered. The console beeped softly, a sound as familiar as my own heartbeat. I traced the cold, smooth glass, my breath fogging a tiny circle on its surface. Out there was a future I would never know, a home built by hands I would never shake. Here, in this humming metal womb, was my entire universe. Beautiful, and so very, very small.
