The air in the attic was thick with dust and memory. Leo traced a finger along the edge of the old steamer trunk, the leather warm from the afternoon sun slanting through the circular window. Inside, nestled in faded velvet, wasn’t the family silver or lost jewels, but something better: his grandfather’s old leather jacket, a set of polished lockpicks, and a faded map of the city, marked with a dozen tiny X’s.

“Every mark,” his grandfather’s voice echoed in his memory, a warm rasp, “is where we helped someone who needed it. The best cons aren’t about taking, kid. They’re about giving something back.”

Leo slipped the jacket on. It smelled of cedar and old adventures. Downstairs, the new landlord’s voice boomed, talking about evictions. Leo looked at the map, then at the landlord’s fancy new safe in the hall. A slow smile spread across his face. It was time for a different kind of heist.
