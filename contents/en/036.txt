The floorboard groaned under my foot. I froze, breath catching in my throat. From the hallway below, the heavy footsteps stopped.

“I know you’re up there.” His voice was a low rumble, devoid of its usual warmth.

My fingers, slick with sweat, tightened around the photo. Mom’s smiling face stared back, a stark contrast to the man now climbing the stairs. The lie I’d swallowed for years—the perfect father, the safe home—shattered with each creaking step. The evidence was here, in this faded picture of a woman he swore had simply left us.

“Come down, sweetheart.” The endearment tasted like ash.

I backed toward the attic window, the night air biting through the glass. Jump or face him? My heart hammered a frantic rhythm against my ribs. There was no safe choice anymore.
