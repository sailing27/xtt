The old oak in the park had always whispered, but only Leo heard it. He pressed his palm to the bark, feeling the hum of ancient stories. “The wind’s changing,” the tree murmured, its voice like rustling parchment. “She’s coming back.”

Across the grass, Maya felt a sudden, inexplicable pull. The air tasted of ozone and forgotten birthdays. She watched the boy at the tree, his head cocked as if listening. A single, luminous leaf spiraled down and landed in her hair.

Leo saw it glow. “It’s for you,” he called out, the words leaving him in a rush he couldn’t explain.

Maya touched the leaf. A cascade of memories-not-hers flooded her mind: a girl in a different time, making the same promise to this rooted giant. “I remember,” she breathed, though she didn’t know how. The oak sighed, content. Its two lost guardians had found each other again.
