The rain fell in sheets, blurring the amber glow of the streetlights. Leo stood under the awning, his heart a frantic drum against his ribs. He’d rehearsed the words a hundred times, but now they were ash in his mouth. Across the slick pavement, under the flickering neon of the closed bakery, stood Maya. Her coat was soaked, her expression unreadable.

“You came,” he called out, the wind stealing half his voice.

“You said it was important.” Her reply was cool, a shard of ice in the downpour.

He stepped into the deluge, the cold water shocking his skin. This was the moment. The space between heartbeats stretched into an eternity. “I lied,” he confessed, the truth finally breaking free. “It wasn’t important. It was everything.”
