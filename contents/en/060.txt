The walls shift when I blink. I know they do, even though Leo insists it’s just the stress. “The maze isn’t alive, Cass,” he says, voice tight. “It’s just code. Bad, glitchy code.”

But I hear it breathing—a low, digital hum in the stone. I counted thirteen turns since the last glyph. Leo says it was nine. His memory is the one glitching, not the labyrinth. I have to believe that. If I’m wrong, then the soft, whispering scrape from the corridor behind us isn’t a server fan. It’s something the maze wrote to hunt us. My palm is slick against the cold wall. The next junction swims before my eyes, pathways re-knitting themselves in the half-light.

“Left,” I say, pulling him. I don’t tell him I chose it because the right-hand path just whispered my name.
