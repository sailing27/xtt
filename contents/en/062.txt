The rain never stopped on Kaelos Prime. It fell in greasy sheets, streaking the viewport with neon reflections from the city below. Lyra’s breath fogged the glass as she watched the transport lanes, a tangled web of crimson taillights.

“Signal’s clean,” a voice crackled in her earpiece. “He’s on the move.”

She didn’t answer, her fingers tracing the scar on her wrist—a souvenir from her last extraction. The mark itched when she was nervous, a phantom reminder of synth-flesh grafts and whispered promises in dark rooms. Down in the arterial glow, a single figure broke from the crowd, moving against the flow.

*Just a kid*, she thought, her stomach tightening. The briefing had said asset. It hadn’t said his eyes would look so much like her brother’s, lost to the same corporate war. The rain kept falling, washing nothing clean.
