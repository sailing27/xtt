The first time I saw a ghost, I was eight and it was my hamster. Mom said I had an overactive imagination. The second time, I was twelve and it was Grandpa. Dad said grief does funny things. Now I’m sixteen, and the trench is full of them. They don’t talk, just stare with that disappointed look.

“You seeing them again, Leo?” Jenkins asked, scraping mud from his boot.

“Just shadows,” I lied, watching a spectral soldier drift through the barbed wire. He was missing half his head, but in a tidy way.

“Good. Keep it that way.” Jenkins lit a cigarette, the smoke mingling with a ghost’s translucent form. “War’s ugly enough without inventing extras.”

I nodded, pretending not to see the one peering over his shoulder, mimicking his every move. It’s easier if they think you’re crazy. It’s harder when you know you’re not.
