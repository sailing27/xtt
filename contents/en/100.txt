The walls shift with a soft, grinding hum. I press my palm against the cold stone, feeling the faint vibration of the code running through it. My father’s final, frantic transmission echoes in my skull: *Find the keystone, before the system resets.*

“Left,” I whisper, tracing a glyph only I can see. The corridor ahead shimmers, reality pixelating at the edges. A wrong turn here doesn’t lead to a dead end—it rewrites you.

A voice, synthesized and cold, filters from an overhead grate. “Subject diverging from prescribed path.”

“I’m making my own,” I mutter, fingers flying over a hidden panel. The air crackles with ozone. One more sequence. The hum rises to a shriek. The wall before me dissolves into light, revealing not an exit, but a vast, glowing core. The labyrinth’s beating heart. And I’m inside it.
