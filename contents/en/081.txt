The attic smelled of cedar and forgotten summers. I was searching for Grandma’s old locket when my fingers brushed the loose floorboard. Beneath it, nestled in dust, lay a small, leather-bound journal I’d never seen. Its cover was cool and cracked.

“Find what you’re looking for, Ellie?” Mom called from the foot of the stairs.

“Just old stuff,” I answered, my voice tight.

I flipped it open. The first page was in Grandma’s elegant script, but the words made no sense. *They never found him. I kept the key.* My breath hitched. Taped to the next page was a faded photograph of our house, with a strange symbol drawn in the corner—the same symbol I’d just noticed carved into my bedroom windowsill.
