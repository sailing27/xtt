The recruitment poster promised glory. What I got was a leaky tent and Sergeant Briggs, whose breath could curdle milk at twenty paces.

“Welcome to paradise, maggots!” he bellowed, gesturing to the mudscape that was Camp Kilter. “Your home until you’re clever or dead.”

My bunkmate, a kid named Finn with eyes too wide for his face, whispered, “I think my boots are dissolving.”

“Organic reclamation,” I muttered, watching a sock vanish into the muck. “We’re helping the earth.”

The first mortar hit at dawn. The sound wasn’t a bang, but a giant ripping the sky in half. Dirt rained down like black snow.

Finn was trembling. “This isn’t like the poster.”

“Posters lie,” I said, brushing grime from my collar. “The font was nice, though. Very bold.”

He stared. Then, he laughed—a sharp, startled sound that was the first honest thing I’d heard in weeks. We picked up our rifles. The sky tore open again.
