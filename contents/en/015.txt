The last thing I remember clearly is the lemon tart. It was perfect, you see—a precise, sun-bright yellow. I didn’t take it. I merely… admired it. Intensely. But when Mrs. Havisham’s prized silver teaspoon vanished from beside her teacup, all eyes swiveled to me. My sticky fingers were, admittedly, circumstantial.

“It was the butler,” I announced, with the confidence of someone who reads a great many detective novels. “He has a grudge against citrus-based desserts.”

The butler, polishing a candlestick, sighed a weary sigh. My sister Elara pinched the bridge of her nose. “You’ve got jam on your chin,” she whispered.

A glint of silver caught my eye from the chandelier overhead. The spoon was there, dangling, as if placed by a very tidy ghost—or a magpie with a sense of humor. I decided not to mention it. The butler’s expression was far too entertaining.
