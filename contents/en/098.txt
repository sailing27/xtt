The last orange was soft in her palm, its skin dimpled like an old face. She’d saved it for three days, a tiny sun against the endless gray of the sea and sky.

“We’ll split it,” she whispered to her brother, his lips cracked with salt.

He shook his head, eyes fixed on the horizon. “You.”

The knife was dull, but it parted the peel, releasing a burst of citrus scent that was almost painful in its sweetness. Juice stung the cuts on her hands. She handed him half, the segments glistening. He took it, his fingers trembling.

As the tartness exploded on her tongue, she didn’t taste hope. She tasted a memory: their kitchen, sunlight through the window, a bowl of fruit on the table. A world that was, for this moment, real again.
