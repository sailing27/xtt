The rain on the windowpane smeared the city lights into bleeding watercolors. Leo traced a finger through the condensation, his own reflection a ghost over the dark street below. *They’re all out there, and none of them know.*

Across the hall, his sister Maya pressed her ear to her bedroom door, the floorboards cold against her cheek. She heard the low murmur of their parents’ voices, a tense, familiar rhythm. *Arguing about money again. Or is it about him?*

In the living room, their mother stared at an unpaid bill, the numbers blurring. The weight of the silence from the children’s rooms felt heavier than any shouted accusation. She didn’t hear the soft click of Leo’s door opening, or the hesitant shuffle of his feet as he moved toward the sound of the fight, a single, crumpled note held tight in his fist.
